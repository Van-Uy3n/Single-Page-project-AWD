About BTS

BTS, which stands for Bangtan Sonyeondan (translation: Bulletproof Boy Scouts) in Korean and Beyond the Scene in English, is a South Korean music group with seven members. They made their debut on June 12, 2013 from the music company Big Hit Entertainment. The members are:

  • RM
  • Jin
  • Suga
  • J-Hope
  • Jimin
  • V
  • Jungkook

They are considered self-producing idols. Although they are not always involved in the creation process, they do participate in most of it. All of the members have helped write, arrange, compose, and/or produce music for the group or themselves.

The group is also known for their energetic choreography. Captivating the audience, they also perform beautiful dances that convey a story.

Often, their music explores the identity of young people or comments on society. From their music, fans–known as ARMY (meaning: Adorable Representative M.C. for Youth)–were inspired with hope because the group helped them find empowerment or a silver lining.

Fans also resonated with and valued their more somber songs. “Spring Day” is their most loved song by the Korean general public because they were touched by the message of longing after a loss and hope

Furthermore, there is the BTS Universe: a universe where the members are characters, who face individual problems, connected by fate. Since the storyline is up for interpretation, ARMYs devise theories. The storyline is present in some of their:

  • Music videos
  • Short films
  • Highlight Reels

Originally, it was hard for the group to be respected without a famous company backing them up.

However, they began to rise to fame in South Korea after they finally won their first award on a music show, “The Show,” for their song “I Need U” in 2015. Then, BTS gained popularity in the Western music market, especially the United States, with their song “DNA” in 2017. This led them to be part of and perform at their first American award show: the American Music Awards.

BTShe group gained more fans, credibility, and prominence domestically and globally. Their dedicated fans are also credited for their rise to fame and achievements. They stream, buy BTS related works and products, create BTS-related content, organize BTS-related events (ex. birthday fundraisers for charity), and more.

Campaigns

Love Myself

In 2017, BTS released and began their Love Yourself series, which comprised of three albums (Love Yourself: Her, Love Yourself: Tear, & Love Yourself: Answer) and one video (Love Yourself: Wonder). Each album journeyed through different types of love: joyful and pure love, painful love and heartbreak, and self-love. The aim of the series was to tell a story about finding and exploring true love, which was realized to be self-love.

The message was to love yourself because accepting your worth and flaws will help you love others. When you can love yourself, you can give that same love to others.

On November 1, 2017, BTS and Big Hit Entertainment partnered with UNICEF (United Nations Children's Fund) in their #ENDviolence campaign in order to spread the message of self-love. #ENDviolence's objective was to eradicate violence against children and teens by providing them safety. It was created to promote protection for young people and devise solutions to end violence.

Thus, the group and its label created the LOVE MYSELF campaign for their partnership with UNICEF. LOVE MYSELF promoted self-love for young children globally. With love, the world can become a better place to live in.

At some concerts, there would be UNICEF and LOVE MYSELF booths set up to teach fans about the cause's message. Fans have contributed to the cause by donating money, sharing their own self-love journeys, and promoting the cause on social media.

BTS pledged to donate 3% of the album sales for the Love Yourself series and all the proceeds made from the campaign's merchandise. Donations made by them and fans are made towards protecting children from:

  • Domestic violence
  • School violence
  • Sexual violence

For more information and updates about the campaign, visit: BTS: Love Myself.

Achievements

  • 2021: Special Presidential Envoy for Future Generations and Culture
  • 2020: James A. Van Fleet Award
  • 2020: Time Entertainer of the Year
  • 2019: Album of the Year at Seoul Music Awards for Love Yourself: Tear
  • 2019: Global Sustainable Future Leaders. The United Nations named BTS this because of their advocation for young people's lives and donations and fundraisers for the Love Myself campaign. Also, ARMY was given this title because of their charitable projects organized on behalf of the group.
  • 2018: Hangwon Order of Cutural Merit (5th class) for spreading South Korean culture globally
  • 2018: Best Music Video at Mnet Asian Awards for "Idol"
  • 2017: Song of the Year at Melon Music Awards for "Spring Day"
  • 2017: Album of the Year for 4th Quarter for Wings at the Gaon Chart Music Awards
  • 2016: Honored as Minister of Culture, Sports and Tourism Commondation at the Korean Popular Culture and Arts Awards (government-ran)
  • 2016: Album of the Year at Melon Music Awards for their album The Most Beautiful Moment in Life: Young Forever. This was an momentous win because it was their first Daesang: a prestigious, grand prize given to those with the greatest achievements in Korea for the year.
  • 2015: World Rookie Award at Gaon Chart Music Awards
  • 2014 Best New Artist at Seoul Music Awards
