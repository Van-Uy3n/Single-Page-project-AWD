About BTS

BTS, which stands for Bangtan Sonyeondan (translation: Bulletproof Boy Scouts) in Korean and Beyond the Scene in English, is a sensational South Korean boy group with seven creative and driven members. They made their debut on June 12, 2013 from the music company Big Hit Entertainment, which is now acting as a music label under HYBE Corporation--an entertainment company Big Hit recently rebranded as. The artistic septet is made up of rappers and vocalists, and the members are: RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook. Although they are not fully involved in the production process or credited as the main producers, they do participate in most of their music. Especially the rappers of the group, all of the members have helped write, arrange, compose, and/or produce most songs for either the group or themselves individually.


The main attraction to BTS has been profound lyrics and concepts because listeners have found them relatable and interesting. While anyone of any background can listen to their songs, the target of their music was originally to connect to the youth and express about the youth's identity and experiences in society. Through their exploration of themes, fans–known as ARMY (meaning: Adorable Representative M.C. for Youth)–were inspired with hope and self-worth because the group helped fans find empowerment or a silver lining; fans also resonated with and valued the more somber songs. Their song “Spring Day” is especially loved by the Korean general public because they were touched by the message of longing after a loss and hope. Furthermore, fans have been captivated by the BTS Universe, an intricate fictional universe where the members are individuals dealing with their own struggles and are connected by fate. The storyline is present in some of their music videos, short films, and hightlight reels. Since the storyline is up for interpretation, there are many different theories the fans have come up with. They have fun theorizing the meanings of symbols, scenes, and more.


Originally, the group came from a small company, which meant it was hard for them to be notable without a famous company backing them up. In addition, there were many other active K-Pop groups that caught the public's interest. Because it was competitive to achieve and maintain fame in the K-Pop industry, it was challenging for BTS to be successful after their debut for a couple of years. However, they began to rise to fame in South Korea after they finally won their first award on a music show, “The Show,” for their song “I Need U” in 2015. Then, BTS gained popularity in the Western music market, especially the United States, with their song “DNA” in 2017. This led them to be part of and perform at their first American award show: the American Music Awards. Over the next following years to the present, the group has gained more fans, credibility, and prominence domestically and globally. Their diligence and dedicated fans are credited for their rise to fame and achievements. The fans are a dedicated, strong community. They stream, buy BTS related works and products, create BTS-related content, organize BTS-related events (ex. birthday fundraisers for charity), and more. Without their fans' determination to promote them and help them break records, they would not have the assistance needed to soar higher and higher and maintain a stable career. The group is forever grateful for their fans’ love and always strive to make them happy with their music.

Campaigns

Love Myself

Starting in the summer of 2017, BTS released and began their Love Yourself series, which comprised of three albums (LOVE YOURSELF: Her, LOVE YOURSELF: Tear, & LOVE YOURSELF: Answer) and one video (LOVE YOURSELF: Wonder). Each album journeyed through different types of love: joyful and cheery love, painful love and heartbreak, and self-love. The aim of the series was to tell a story about finding and exploring true love, which was realized to be self-love. The message was to love yourself because accepting your worth and flaws would help you love others. When you can love yourself, you can give that same love to others.


On November 1, 2017, BTS and Big Hit Entertainment partnered with UNICEF in its #ENDviolence campaign in order to spread their message of self-love.The #ENDviolence campaign's objective was to eradicate violence against children and teens by providing them safety. It was created to promote protection for young people and come up with solutions to ending violence, such as supporting education and economic support. Thus, the group and its label created the LOVE MYSELF campaign for their partnership with UNICEF. The LOVE MYSELF campaign promoted self-love for young children globally. With love, the world can become a better place to live in. Through the campaign, BTS and its label pledged to donate 3% of the album sales for the Love Yourself series and all the proceeds made from the campaign's merchandise. At some concerts, there would be UNICEF and Love Myself booths set up to teach fans about the cause's message and encourage them to join. In addition, fans have contributed to the cause by donating money to the campaign's fundraiser, sharing their own struggles and journeys to self-love, and promoting the cause on social media


For more information and updates about the campaign, visit: BTS: Love Myself.
